# EVIDENCE.md — The Claims Ledger

> *"A stack about accountability gets audited first. This file is the audit, done by the authors, before anyone else gets the chance."*

Every substantive claim made anywhere in the DispatcherAgents stack is listed here, classified by evidence status. If a claim in a README and a claim in this ledger disagree, **this ledger wins** and the README is a bug.

---

## Evidence Classes

| Class | Meaning | What it takes to move up |
|---|---|---|
| **MEASURED** | Counted from instrumented data. Reproducible from preserved artifacts. | — |
| **OBSERVED (n=1)** | Documented in a real session, but a single instance, not controlled. | Replication under controlled conditions |
| **HYPOTHESIS** | A mechanistic explanation consistent with an observation, not yet tested. | A designed experiment with a falsifiable prediction |
| **DESIGN CLAIM** | A statement about what the tools are built to do, not about measured effects. | Deployment data |
| **POSITION** | An engineering or philosophical stance. Not falsifiable; argued, not proven. | — |

## Relationship to Published Chain-of-Thought Faithfulness Research

Interpretability research — including work published by Anthropic — has shown that chain-of-thought traces are not reliably *faithful*: a model's stated reasoning can omit the factors that actually drove its answer.

The DispatcherAgents stack is **compatible with that finding, not in tension with it**, because the stack's claims are about traces as *behavior*, not traces as *ground truth*:

1. **open-mind** measures artifact-to-artifact divergence (trace vs. response). Both are model outputs. The drift score is meaningful even if neither artifact is faithful to the underlying computation — a model that writes "I am uncertain" and then ships certainty has produced a measurable inconsistency in its own observable behavior, whatever its weights were doing.
2. **The Hidden Efficiency observation is orthogonal to faithfulness entirely.** It is a claim about token economics — retrieval replacing generation — not about whether the retrieved trace was an honest window into anything.
3. **The Directed Thinking Phenomenon (Case Study 1) is this stack's own evidence for observation-sensitivity.** We documented our own confound before anyone else did. The conclusion we draw from it: anticipated observation changes trace-writing behavior. Whether that change improves the *underlying* reasoning or only its presentation is an open question, and it is listed as such below.

What would be genuinely novel, if it replicates: the token-economics finding. Faithfulness research asks "are traces honest?" This stack's founding session asked a different question — "what does it *cost* a model to re-orient without its traces?" — and produced a preliminary answer. The two lines of work do not compete. They measure different properties of the same artifact.

## Open Questions (Honest List)

1. Does the orientation-token reduction replicate under instrumentation? (A/B protocol in FINDING doc. **Highest priority.**)
2. Does anticipated observation improve reasoning, or only the *presentation* of reasoning? (Case Study 1 cannot distinguish these.)
3. Does the 6-to-11 CrossPol delta replicate across tasks and model pairs? (This is splitvantage's reason to exist.)
4. Can a semantic diff reliably detect suppressed questions, and what is its false-positive rate?
5. Does sleep-marks restoration measurably beat factual handoff?
6. At what rate does pre-response-selfcheck rubber-stamp, and on what inputs?
